Why our produce is super nutritious

Don't trust us, trust the science. You may wonder why if you've been eating fruits and vegetables that you have not experienced their miraculous health benefits. And you may also wonder why we can confidently claim our produce is truly life-changing. We supply our plants with high density nutrients delivered from our proprietary aquifer. Our produce matches the nutrient profile of fruit and vegetables that grew on earth before modern agricultural methods were developed. Thanks to our scientific evidence derived from archaeological data, we can prove our food is nearly identical to that which humans consumed during most of our evolution. The food you find in the produce section of a grocery store has nearly nothing in common with that grown just a few hundred years ago other than its appearance.

A single serving of our delicious produce contains more vitamins, minerals, and essential nutrients than four times the amount of produce from a typical supermarket. Modern agricultural techniques have rendered fruits and vegetables so depleted in essential nutrients that they have the nutrient profile as sawdust, along with a flavor to match. If we were termites, that might be OK, but humans cannot thrive on a diet of wood. Flavor = Nutrition...but not always!

From the moment you bite into our produce, you will never want to eat any grown using conventional agricultural methods. It's no wonder kids hate eating fruits and vegetables today. Their palates have been subjected to bland natural foods and at the same time to the over-the-top artificial flavor profiles infused into processed foods.

Produce today is optimized for the following attributes:

  1. Durability during transportation
  2. Maximum yield
  3. Appearance

All of these attributes are optimized to the exclusion of the nutritional content and flavor. After all, nutrient content can only be measured directly with expensive instrumentation which consumers don't possess. Flavor can only be measured after taking a bite of the produce which you can't do prior to its purchase. Your body knows how to detect nutrient deficient foods. If you feel tired, bloated, and foggy-headed 30 minutes after you've eaten, then the food you ate was not nutritious. You'll be uncomfortably full but not satiated.
